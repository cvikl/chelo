TRIGLAV — VIDEO SCRIPT (5 minutes)
====================================

Style: Fast-paced, TikTok/YouTube energy. Cuts every 3-5 seconds.
Music: Lo-fi or upbeat electronic underneath throughout.


---
[0:00 - 0:30] HOOK + INTRO (Filmed outside, mountains in background)
---

PERSON ON CAMERA (walking toward camera, energetic):
"What if I told you that half the climate articles you read about the Alps... are wrong?"

*quick cut*

"Some say glaciers are fine. Some say snow hasn't changed. Some even say the Alps aren't warming."

*cut to dramatic mountain shot*

"But what does the actual satellite data say?"

*cut back to person*

"We built a tool that takes any climate article, pulls REAL satellite imagery, runs it through AI... and tells you exactly what's true and what's not."

"This is Triglav."

*TITLE CARD: TRIGLAV - Alpine Climate Fact-Checker*


---
[0:30 - 1:15] THE PROBLEM (Voiceover + screen recording of articles/headlines)
---

VOICEOVER (show scrolling through climate skeptic headlines):
"Climate misinformation about the Alps is everywhere. Articles claiming glaciers are stable. Blog posts saying snow cover hasn't changed. Opinion pieces denying any warming."

"The data to debunk these claims exists — satellite imagery, weather stations, glacier models — but it's scattered across dozens of databases and takes expertise to interpret."

"So we asked: what if we could automate the fact-checking? What if an AI could read an article, figure out what claims it makes, fetch the actual satellite data, and compare them — in seconds?"


---
[1:15 - 2:00] HOW IT WORKS (Voiceover + architecture diagram or animation)
---

VOICEOVER (show simple flow diagram):
"Here's how Triglav works."

"Step one: you paste a climate article. Our orchestrator — powered by a large language model — reads it and extracts every verifiable claim. Location, time period, what the article says about temperature, snow, glaciers, vegetation."

"Step two: we dispatch five specialized agents in parallel. Each one fetches REAL data."

(show each agent briefly)
"Temperature and precipitation — real weather data from ERA5, going back to 1940."
"Snow cover — actual snow depth records plus Sentinel-2 satellite snow maps."
"Glacier extent — we fetch real Sentinel-2 satellite tiles and run them through a deep learning segmentation model trained on Alpine glaciers."
"Vegetation — real NDVI satellite imagery plus vegetation line analysis using elevation data."

"Step three: we compare what the article claims versus what the data actually shows. And we give a verdict: verified, warning, or misleading."


---
[2:00 - 3:30] LIVE DEMO (Screen recording of the product)
---

VOICEOVER (show the actual app):
"Let me show you."

*click "Aletsch Tourism Review" button*

"Here's an article about the Aletsch Glacier region. It makes claims about temperature, snow, glaciers, and vegetation. Let's see what's real."

*show progress bar + Greta game briefly*

"While our agents are fetching real satellite data... you can play a little game."

*results load, show annotated article*

"And here are the results. See how the article is highlighted? Red means the claim is misleading. Yellow is a warning. Green means verified."

*click a red highlight*

"This claim says vegetation hasn't changed. But look — real Sentinel-2 NDVI data shows an 11 percent increase in greening since 2017. The satellite images are right here. You can see the difference."

*click another highlight*

"This one says temperatures haven't risen. But ERA5 data shows warming of nearly 2 degrees per decade. Here's the actual graph — every dot is a real temperature measurement."

*scroll to show the map and verdict summary*

"And down here you can see the full breakdown. Four claims flagged as misleading, backed by real satellite evidence."


---
[3:30 - 4:15] THE TECH (Voiceover + quick cuts of code/data)
---

VOICEOVER (show brief flashes of code, satellite tiles, model output):
"Under the hood, this is all real data. No mock-ups."

"We pull Sentinel-2 satellite imagery from Element84's Earth Search — completely free, no authentication needed."

"For glaciers, we use a trained UNet segmentation model with a Jaccard score of 0.89, running on 16-channel inputs — 13 spectral bands plus elevation, slope, and elevation change data."

"Temperature and precipitation come from the Open-Meteo ERA5 reanalysis archive — weather data going back to 1940."

"We even integrated Google Earth Engine for land surface temperature visualization."

"Every chart, every GIF, every number you see — it comes from real measurements, not made-up data."


---

---

"Journalists need tools to verify claims quickly. Readers need a way to check what they're reading. And policymakers need evidence they can trust."

"Triglav puts the power of satellite data into everyone's hands."

====================================
PRODUCTION NOTES
====================================

TOTAL: ~5 minutes

Shot list:
- Outdoor intro: person walking toward camera with mountains behind (15 sec)
- B-roll: dramatic mountain/glacier shots (grab from Pexels or own footage)
- Screen recording: full demo flow with the three articles
- Code flashes: quick 1-2 second shots of the codebase
- Satellite imagery: Sentinel-2 tiles, NDSI snow maps
- Outdoor outro: same location as intro

Screen recording tips:
- Use the Aletsch article for the main demo (best data coverage)
- Click through 2-3 highlights to show the popup with charts
- Show the Greta game briefly (funny moment)
- Zoom into the charts when talking about specific data

Editing style:
- Quick cuts (2-4 seconds max per shot)
- Zoom-ins on key UI elements when mentioning them
- Lower-third text for key stats ("11% greening increase", "+2°C/decade")
- Subtle screen shake on dramatic reveals
- Sound effects on verdict reveals (ding for verified, buzz for misleading)
